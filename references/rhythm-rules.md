# Rhythm Rules

## Goal

Keep clip timing and merged pacing usable for short-video storytelling.

## Single-clip timing guidance

### Transition / atmosphere clip
- usually 2–4 seconds
- should not over-explain

### Main action clip
- usually 4–6 seconds
- enough time for visible motion and emotional read

### Dialogue-bearing clip
- usually 4–6 seconds
- needs space for speech plus reaction after speech
- avoid collapsing the clip to only the spoken phrase unless explicitly desired

## Merge pacing rules

When merging clips:
- confirm each clip has a reason to exist in sequence
- preserve the emotional transition between neighboring clips
- avoid abrupt audio scene resets
- if one clip contains speech, leave some non-speech breathing room before or after the line

## Anti-patterns

Avoid these:
- a talking clip that becomes only a 2-second fragment when the surrounding narrative expects 6 seconds
- every clip using different ambience logic
- merged output sounding like separate experiments glued together
